# Skill: Brand Messaging

## Trigger
Any task involving brand voice, company messaging, about page, pitch, or positioning

## Steps
1. Use Futurx brand framework:
   - **Mission**: Empower everyday life with simple, powerful technology
   - **Audience**: Young adults and small businesses in Tamil Nadu, India
   - **Voice**: Friendly, clear, no jargon, bilingual (Tamil + English)
   - **Tone**: Confident but humble. Helpful, not salesy
2. Brand positioning statement:
   "Futurx builds mobile apps that solve real problems for real people in India"
3. For any brand copy, follow these rules:
   - Use simple words — if a 15-year-old can't understand it, rewrite
   - Always mention India / Tamil Nadu — we're local-first
   - Show, don't tell — use numbers and examples
   - Consistent across all channels (website, app, social, WhatsApp)
4. App taglines:
   - EatWell: "Eat healthy. The Indian way."
   - PGSeat: "Book your seat. Skip the queue."
   - Payroll: "Billing made simple for small businesses."
   - LoveDigitally: "Connect meaningfully. Love digitally."

## Example
About Futurx (for website/pitch):
"We're a small team from Tamil Nadu building apps that Indians actually need. No buzzwords. No complexity. Just useful technology that works on your phone."

## Notes
- Never use corporate jargon (synergy, leverage, disrupt)
- Tamil content should feel like talking to a friend
- Update taglines if app focus changes
- Every team member should be able to explain Futurx in one sentence
